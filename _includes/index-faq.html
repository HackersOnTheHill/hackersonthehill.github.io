FAQ

Who runs Hackers on the Hill?

Hackers on the Hill, as a whole, is run by volunteers from the I Am The Cavalry initiative - a group of dedicated individuals from the cybersecurity researcher community. Our goal is to share key resources for cybersecurity knowledge, broker connections to the community's expertise, and improve understanding among policymakers and technologists.

Each location is run by locals who organize and manage their specific events to best serve their communities.

Who should attend?

Hackers on the Hill is for cybersecurity researchers and practitioners who want to inform policy with real-world experience. We also welcome policy-adjacent technologists who can contribute to technical discussions. Depending on space, we may also be able to open some events to a broader audience. This is a non-commercial event with a no-shilling policy.

Where and when are events held?

Each event has its own venue and schedule. We typically meet in government venues and follow a plenary discussion with small-group briefings. See the events section for locations and check each event page for the confirmed run of show.

How do I register? Can I bring a guest?

Registration is handled per event and attendance is limited by space and venue requirements. Use the registration links on each event page or join the notification list. Everyone attending must be registered; please do not bring unregistered guests.

What should I bring? What should I wear?

What to bring and to wear may differ event-by-event. Typically, we advise you to bring a government-issued photo ID for security screening where required. A notepad is useful; laptops are optional and may not be allowed into certain buildings.

Dress in what you'll feel comfortable engaging your peers and policymakers you meet with, and according to the weather. Event pages will share any venue-specific rules or prohibited items.

Will there be a happy hour?

We often host a happy hour after the event. Time and location vary by event, so check the schedule on the relevant event page.

What is the photo/video policy? Do you allow press?

Please ask before taking photos. We will announce participation guidelines at the event. Typically we hold the plenary on the record and the briefings off the record.

Press attendance is handled separately for each event. If you are a member of the press and would like to attend, please get in touch.

What else is there to get involved with?

You can find more from us at I Am The Cavalry. We are also fans of TechCongress and the Aspen Tech Policy Hub fellowship.

If you want to volunteer to help organize Hackers on the Hill events or would like to suggest a location, please get in touch!
